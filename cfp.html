Submission Guidelines

Paper Types

Full Papers (6-8 pages)

Original research contributions with substantial technical content and experimental validation.

Short Papers (3-4 pages)

Work-in-progress, preliminary results, or position papers that present novel ideas.

Industry Papers (4-6 pages)

Practical experiences, case studies, and industrial applications of energy-efficient computing.

Topics of Interest

AI and Machine Learning

  • Energy-efficient neural network architectures
  • Green AI algorithms and training methods
  • Power-aware machine learning frameworks
  • Sustainable deep learning practices
  • Energy optimization in AI inference

High-Performance Computing

  • Energy-efficient HPC system design
  • Power management and scheduling strategies
  • Sustainable datacenter operations
  • Green computing metrics and benchmarks
  • Renewable energy integration in HPC

Quantum Computing

  • Energy considerations in quantum systems
  • Quantum algorithms for energy optimization
  • Sustainable quantum computing architectures
  • Quantum advantage for sustainability problems
  • Hybrid classical-quantum energy solutions

Cross-Cutting Themes

  • Carbon footprint assessment and reduction
  • Life-cycle analysis of computing systems
  • Policy and regulatory aspects
  • Economic models for sustainable computing
  • Regional perspectives and challenges

Formatting Requirements

  • Format: IEEE Conference format (double-column)
  • Language: English
  • Anonymization: Papers should be anonymized for double-blind review
  • File Format: PDF only
  • Page Limits: Include references and appendices within the page limit
  • Template: Download IEEE template

Review Process

All submissions will undergo a rigorous double-blind peer review process. Each paper will be reviewed by at least three experts in the field. Reviews will focus on:

  • Technical quality and novelty
  • Relevance to workshop themes
  • Experimental validation and results
  • Clarity of presentation
  • Potential impact on the field
