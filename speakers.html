Carla Osthoff

Latin America Perspective • 16:30 - 17:10

National Laboratory for Scientific Computing (LNCC), Brazil

Dr. Carla Osthoff holds a degree in Electrical Engineering from the Pontifical Catholic University of Rio de Janeiro, as well as master's and PhD degrees in Systems and Computer Engineering from the Federal University of Rio de Janeiro. She initially worked in the field of high-performance computing as an electronics engineer on hardware development projects for distributed parallel multiprocessors, collaborating between the Brazilian Center for Physics Research and the Fermi National Laboratory (USA).

Currently, she is a Senior Technologist III at LNCC and works in the research area of High Performance Computing, is a professor in the Multidisciplinary Postgraduate Program at LNCC, coordinates the National Center for High Performance Processing (CENAPAD) at LNCC, is a member of the Technical-Scientific body of the Santos Dumont Supercomputer Advisory Committee, coordinates the High Performance Processing Sector at LNCC, and has been co-coordinator of the Santos Dumont Supercomputer Summer School since 2011.

She also served as coordinator of the Regional Performance Act Commission of the State of Rio de Janeiro from 2020 to 2024, is a member of the Executive Board of the Advanced Computing System of Latin America and the Caribbean (SCALAC) since 2019, is a collaborating researcher - Centre de Recherche INRIA Bordeaux Sud-Ouest (FRA) and served as Program Chair of SBAC-PAD 2024 and CARLA 2024.

Talk Topics:

  • Machine learning-based techniques for energy-efficient resource allocation
  • Energy-Delay Product optimization in supercomputing environments
  • Hardware resource allocation optimization using Extra Trees Regressor
  • Sustainable HPC practices in Latin America
Photo

Dieter Kranzlmüller

Energy Efficiency as a Key to Sustainable HPC and AI • 14:00 - 14:40

Leibniz Supercomputing Centre (LRZ) and LMU Munich

Biography:

Dieter Kranzlmüller is Director of the Leibniz Supercomputing Centre (LRZ) and Professor of Computer Science at LMU Munich. He leads one of Europe's most powerful HPC facilities, where his work drives the evolution of scientific computing. Beyond operating large-scale systems such as SuperMUC-NG, his focus lies on integrating next-generation technologies — from AI to quantum computing — into classical HPC environments. Under his leadership, LRZ is shaping into a comprehensive Future Computing center, equipping researchers in Germany and across Europe with the infrastructure to address today's most complex scientific and societal challenges.

Key Focus Areas:

  • Energy efficiency strategies for HPC and AI systems
  • Innovative cooling concepts including hot-water cooling
  • Energy-aware system design and renewable energy integration
  • Sustainable computing ecosystem development
  • Integration of AI and quantum computing in HPC environments
Photo

John DeSantis

US Perspective • 09:10 - 09:50

Texas Advanced Computing Center (TACC)

John DeSantis is a Large Scale Systems administrator at TACC and lead of Vista, TACC's NVIDIA Grace cluster. Prior to TACC, John entered the world of HPC at the University of South Florida in November 2011. John has worked professionally with Linux and FreeBSD operating systems since 1999 (he has the gray beard to prove it).

Talk Topics:

  • Comparative power usage analysis between ARM and x86 CPUs
  • NVIDIA Grace cluster architecture and performance
  • Energy efficiency in large-scale HPC systems
  • Systems administration for sustainable computing
Photo

Lubomir Riha

Europe Perspective • 11:30 - 12:10

IT4Innovations National Supercomputing Center

Lubomir Riha, Ph.D., is Head of the Infrastructure Research Lab at IT4Innovations National Supercomputing Center. He holds Ph.D. degrees in Electrical Engineering from Czech Technical University in Prague and Computer Science from Bowie State University, USA. Previously, he was a research scientist at George Washington University's High-Performance Computing Lab.

He serves as principal investigator on several major European HPC projects including EUPEX, DARE, and SEANERGYS, focusing on energy-efficient computing and exascale system development. He is the developer of the energy-efficient MERIC suite and co-founder of the GPU-accelerated ESPRESO FEM library. His research spans performance optimization, energy-aware software development, and GPU acceleration for scientific applications.

Research Areas:

  • Energy-efficient HPC systems and software
  • Performance optimization of parallel applications
  • GPU acceleration and modern many-core architectures
  • European HPC sustainability initiatives

Pedro Mário Cruz e Silva

Solution Architect for Higher-Education and Research • 14:40 - 15:10

NVIDIA - Latin America Region

Pedro Mário Cruz e Silva did his BSc (1995), and MSc (1998) at Federal University of Pernambuco (UFPE), he also did his DSc in 2004 at PUC-Rio. He created the Computational Geophysics Group at PUC-Rio where he worked for 15 years as Manager, during this period was responsible for several Software Development and R&D projects for Geophysics with strong focus on innovation. He also finished an MBA in 2015 at Getúlio Vargas Foundation (FGV/RJ).

Currently is the Solution Architect for Higher-Education and Research in the Latin America Region at NVIDIA, bringing extensive experience in computational geophysics and research infrastructure to drive innovation in GPU computing and AI acceleration across Latin American educational and research institutions.

Talk Topics:

  • Next-generation data center architectures for AI reasoning
  • Energy-efficient HPC through Blackwell GPU and Grace CPU
  • Advanced cooling and thermal efficiency in datacenters
  • AI-driven optimization for sustainable infrastructure
